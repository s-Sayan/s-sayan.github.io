Introduction

Welcome to my personal webpage! Currently, I am a Swiss Government Excellence Fellow at the Department of Theoretical Physics, University of Geneva. I am a PhD candidate at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Pune. I am also a visiting researcher at the Astronomy and Astrophysics department of Raman Research Institute (RRI), Bengaluru.

My area of research is theoretical and observational cosmology. Specifically, I study the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB), the most ancient light of the universe. The discovery of the CMB is one of the most amazing discoveries in recent science. Basically, we took a snapshot of the Big Bang.

Research

  • Bayesian estimation of our local motion from the Planck-2018 CMB temperature map
    S. Saha, S. Shaikh, S. Mukherjee, T. Souradeep, and B. D. Wandelt
    The motion of our reference frame can be inferred from the CMB Dipole in mK level. But if there is some contribution from the intrinsic dipole, we won't be able to discriminate between these two. So we infer the velocity from the neighboring mode correlation in the small-scale CMB. We jointly infer the 'a_{lm}' and the power spectrum in a Bayesian approach, where we explore the posterior distribution using a Hamiltonian sampler that we have developed.

  • Dark Matter Halos under the spotlight of CMB Lensing [In preparation]
    S. Saha, Louis Legrand, and Julien Carron
    The abundances of the galaxy clusters tell us about the expansion history of the universe. To know the mass calibration, we need to measure the masses of these dark matter halos. We use the lensing of the cosmic microwave background to probe the mass profile of these clusters. The convergence profile of such galaxy clusters is directly connected to the mass of the clusters. We use the maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimator (Carron et al. 2017) to estimate the convergence. This improves the Signal-to-Noise Ratio of mass measurement by 13% for a CMB-S4 like experiment. We provide theoretical forecasts and also apply our estimator on simulations.
